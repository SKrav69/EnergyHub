# EnergyHub Vision

> **The Operating System for Autonomous Homes**

---

## Mission

EnergyHub exists to reduce the cognitive load of living in a modern smart home.

Modern homes are becoming more complex: solar panels, batteries, inverters, dynamic tariffs, EV charging, heat pumps, sensors, dashboards, notifications and cloud services.

EnergyHub turns that complexity into calm, predictable automation.

The goal is not to make people manage more technology.

The goal is to let the home manage itself.

---

## Core Vision

People should not manage their home.

Their home should manage itself.

EnergyHub continuously coordinates the home energy ecosystem so homeowners do not have to think about batteries, tariffs, solar production, charging schedules, inverter settings or routine energy decisions.

---

## Long-Term Vision

The next generation of homes will become autonomous.

Smart homes connect devices.

Autonomous homes make decisions.

EnergyHub is building the software foundation for that future.

---

## Product Promise

EnergyHub gives homeowners peace of mind.

It quietly manages routine energy decisions in the background while keeping the people in the house comfortable, informed and in control.

The best result is not a dashboard full of numbers.

The best result is a homeowner who almost never has to think about the energy system.

---

## Core Principle

> **We optimize for people, not for kilowatt-hours.**

Energy efficiency matters.

Cost savings matter.

Resilience matters.

But comfort, safety, simplicity and peace of mind come first.

Optimization should never make life harder for the people living in the house.

---

## Autonomous Home

EnergyHub is not just a Home Assistant add-on.

Home Assistant, MQTT, Zigbee, Matter, ESPHome, inverters, batteries, sensors and smart devices are infrastructure.

EnergyHub is the operating system layer that coordinates them into one understandable system.

The homeowner should interact with the home, not with every device separately.

---

## Human-Centric Design

Technology exists to improve people's lives.

Every automation should reduce mental effort, not increase it.

Every new feature should remove one more decision from the homeowner.

The platform should become calmer as it becomes smarter.

---

## Comfort Before Savings

EnergyHub always seeks the best balance between:

* Comfort
* Reliability
* Resilience
* Energy efficiency
* Financial savings

The system should never maximize short-term savings or profit if doing so would reduce comfort, safety or backup readiness.

---

## Simplicity

Complex automation should remain hidden.

Users should interact with simple concepts such as:

* Summer Mode
* Winter Mode
* Away Mode
* Vacation Mode
* Panic Mode
* Manual Override

The complexity stays inside EnergyHub.

---

## Local First

The home must continue operating even without Internet connectivity.

Cloud services are optional.

Core automation should remain local.

---

## Vendor Independent

EnergyHub should support multiple vendors and technologies.

Examples include:

* PowMr
* Deye
* Victron
* Growatt
* LuxPower
* JK BMS
* Daly BMS
* Shelly
* ESPHome
* Zigbee
* Matter

Changing hardware should not require replacing the platform.

---

## Final Thought

EnergyHub should become the software foundation for autonomous homes.

A modern home should behave as one coordinated system, not as a collection of independent devices.

People should spend their time living, not managing devices.
