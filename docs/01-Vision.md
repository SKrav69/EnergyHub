# EnergyHub Vision

> **Making Smart Homes Truly Autonomous**

**Core principle:** We optimize for people, not for kilowatt-hours.

---

## Mission

EnergyHub is an open-source Smart Home Energy Operating System built on top of Home Assistant.

Its mission is to make modern homes truly autonomous by reducing manual interaction, automating routine decisions, and intelligently optimizing energy usage without compromising the comfort, safety, or lifestyle of the people living in the house.

The homeowner defines the desired behavior once.

EnergyHub continuously performs the routine work automatically.

---

## Vision

We believe that people should not have to manage their home.

Their home should manage itself.

EnergyHub continuously monitors the home ecosystem, predicts future conditions, and makes intelligent decisions that maximize comfort while minimizing energy costs.

Technology should become nearly invisible.

Users should simply enjoy living in a comfortable, efficient, and resilient home.

---

## Human-Centric Design

Technology exists to improve people's lives.

Every automation should prioritize the comfort, safety, and convenience of the people living in the house.

Energy savings are important, but they are not the final goal.

The final goal is a comfortable, safe, simple, and efficient life inside the house.

---

## Comfort Before Savings

EnergyHub always seeks the best balance between:

- Comfort
- Reliability
- Energy efficiency
- Financial savings

The system should never maximize short-term profit if doing so would reduce comfort or leave the house without sufficient backup power.

Optimization should never compromise quality of life.

---

## Automation First

Routine decisions should not require human interaction.

EnergyHub automates:

- Solar energy management
- Battery charging and discharging
- Grid import and export
- Electric vehicle charging
- Heat pump operation
- Smart appliances
- Dynamic electricity tariffs
- Net Billing optimization
- Notifications and alerts

---

## Simplicity

Complex automation should remain hidden.

Users interact with simple modes:

- Summer Mode
- Winter Mode
- Away Mode
- Vacation Mode
- Panic Mode

The complexity remains inside EnergyHub.

---

## Explainable Decisions

Every important decision should be understandable.

Whenever EnergyHub changes system behavior, users should be able to see:

- What happened
- Why it happened
- Expected benefits

Automation should build trust, not mystery.

---

## Local First

The home must continue working even without Internet connectivity.

Cloud services are optional.

The house should remain operational locally.

---

## Vendor Independent

EnergyHub should support multiple hardware vendors.

Examples include:

- PowMr
- Deye
- Victron
- Growatt
- LuxPower
- JK BMS
- Daly BMS
- Shelly
- ESPHome
- Zigbee
- Matter

Replacing hardware should never require replacing the platform.

---

## Modular by Design

Every device should be implemented as an independent module.

Adding support for new hardware should extend the platform rather than redesign it.

---

## Open Platform

EnergyHub is designed as an open ecosystem.

Developers should be able to extend the platform with new integrations, automations, dashboards, and optimization algorithms.

---

## Long-Term Vision

EnergyHub aims to become a universal Smart Home Energy Operating System capable of managing residential energy infrastructure and smart home devices.

The platform will continuously optimize the home by balancing comfort, resilience, energy efficiency, and operating costs.

The ultimate goal is simple:

**People should not manage their home.**

**Their home should manage itself.**
